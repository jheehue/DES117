Short Biography

EXO (Egg-So), is a South Korean-Chinese boy band that is based in Seoul, Korea. Formed by an Enterntaiment company (Known as S.M. Entertainment), the group debuted as twelve members. In addition, the group was separated into two subgroups, EXO-K and EXO-M, performing music in both Korean and Mandarin, respectively. Members in EXO-K include: Oh Sehun, Kim Joonmyun(Suho), Do Kyungsoo(D.O), Kim Jongin(Kai), Park Chanyeol, Byun Baekhyun. Members in EXO-M include: Kim Minseok(Xiumin), Kim Jongdae(Chen), Huang Zitao(Tao), Lu Han, Wu Yifan(Kris), Zhang Yixing(Lay).

EXO took a hit globally with the release of their "GROWL" and "XOXO" album in 2013. "XOXO" was a critical commercial success, winning both at the 28th Golden Disk Awards and the 15th Mnet Asian Music Awards. From this era on, EXO was dubbed "King of Male Idol Groups". For their third album, released in 2014 named "Overdose", EXO were ranked as the most influential celebrities by Forbes Korea. In 2015, EXO broke their previous record with the release of their studio album "EXODUS". EXODUS's title track, named "Call Me Baby", became the first and only Kpop track to enter the Canadian HOT 100. As the second released album of "EXODUS" was released as "Love Me Right", EXO later went on to sell more than one million copies, earning them "double million sellers". At last, their fourth album of "Sing For You" (2015) broke EXODUS' first week sales. As of June 2016, EXO takes a big hit with their double track, named "Lucky One" and "Monster".

Unfortunately, on May 2014, Wu Yifan(Kris) filed a lawsuit against S.M. Entertainment to be removed from the group. He felt unequal and unjust treatment between the Korean and Chinese members. In addition, Kris claimed that he was being treated as a product, not an artist. In October 2014, Luhan followed suit due to medical and clinical issues. Following their departure, Huang Zitao suspended his activities in 2015, subsequently followed suit in August 2015. Despite the departure of three of EXO's former members, the group continued to debut as nine members since the "EXODUS" era.
